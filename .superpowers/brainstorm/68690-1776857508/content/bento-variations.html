C1 — Two-Column Bento

Left column is a fixed info stack (metrics, personal, family). Right column holds tabbed deep content (fees, grades, etc.). Everything visible at once — no scrolling to find contacts or metrics. Best for wide screens.

C2 — Single-Page Accordion

No tabs at all. Everything lives on one scrollable page as collapsible sections. Metrics at top, then expandable cards for details, fees, grades, report cards. Section headers show inline badges (e.g. "2 unpaid") so you know what needs attention without expanding.

C3 — Contact Strip + Metrics + Tabs

Minimal, typographic header. Below it: a full-width contact strip with parent cards, then metric cards, then a tabbed card for deep content. Tabs get notification badges (e.g. "2" on Fees). Clean, spacious, modern feel with strong visual hierarchy.

What's different between these:
C1 — Two-column layout. Info pinned left, tabs right. Best for power users on wide screens.
C2 — No tabs. Everything in collapsible accordions. Single scrollable page. Most discoverable.
C3 — Contact strip + metrics up top, tabs below. Balanced between scanability and depth. Best all-rounder.
